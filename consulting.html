We believe in a highly conscious race and we strive to help accelerate the evolution of human consciousness by illuminating the path of self-awareness, self-love, self-trust, and self-empowerment

How I help create the next generation of excellence

As a former Product Designer, Engineer, and Program Manager in several high-tech companies, I know firsthand that our ability to process massive amounts of information, synthesize it quickly, and produce elegant and innovative solutions is essential for maintaining market share in the fast-paced and competitive world we live in.

Even though creativity is essential for our ability to solve complex problems, few organizations understand how to train people to be more creative. However, recent understanding of neuroscience, physiology, and epigenetics are revealing unprecedented insight on how to attain upper ranges of cognition, creativity, and overall human experience.

Change Your Mind – Create New Results is an 8-hour hands-on program created by Dr Joe Dispenza; New York Times bestselling author of Breaking the Habit of Being Yourself, and You Are The Placebo. This course has been specifically designed for organizations and business that are interested in cultivating a collaborative, present, and fully engaged workforce.

Organizational transformation starts from within

Cultures in the workplace are created by the collective behaviors of individual contributors and their leaders. When each individual takes ownership of their own state of being – regardless of the existing environment – that’s when the collective mindset starts to change.

In this program individuals learn how to harness the incredible power of the mind-body connection to produce long lasting states of productivity and awareness.

In this course our participants learn:

The biological signature of living in a state of stress/survival

How to optimize the state of being to foster creativity

How to sustain heightened levels of awareness/presence

Proven tools to create new habitual patterns of behaviors

Several neurological and physiological models of change
